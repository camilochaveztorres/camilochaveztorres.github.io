Project Overview

Crimson Keep was developed as part of a team project at The Game Assembly. The game is a dark action platformer with combat-focused gameplay and a gothic atmosphere. My work was centered on HUD and other player-facing systems that supported clarity, feedback and interaction during gameplay.

My Contributions

HUD

I worked on the HUD, including the presentation of health, ammo and other player-facing information. The goal was to make important gameplay information readable and clear during action-heavy moments.

Collectables

I worked on collectable-related gameplay elements that supported interaction and feedback for the player. This was part of making the moment-to-moment gameplay feel more readable and rewarding.

Destroyable Tiles

I implemented or worked on destroyable tiles that added interaction to the environment. This helped reinforce gameplay feedback and made the world feel more reactive to the player's actions.

Working in TGE

One of the most rewarding parts of the project was working in TGE, which was a completely new engine for me. It was fun to learn a new workflow, understand how the engine was structured, and build gameplay features within that environment.

Feature Highlights

HUD

HUD system showing player health and ammo feedback during combat.

Collectables

Collectables interacting with the player and triggering gameplay feedback.

Destroyable Tiles

Environment tiles reacting to player attacks and changing the level layout.

Additional Contributions

In addition to programming work, I also contributed some voice acting and created sound effects for certain gameplay elements, including the arrow shots. These small contributions helped support the overall atmosphere and feedback of the game.
