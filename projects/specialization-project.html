City Simulation

A systems-focused simulation project built in a custom C++ engine, centered around AI behavior, actor needs, schedules, points of interest, and world resource flow.

Role

Gameplay / AI / Systems Programmer

Engine

Custom C++ Engine

Focus

Simulation Systems, AI, World Interaction

Tech

C++, AI, Custom Systems, Debug Tools

Motivation & Goal

My goal with this project was to build a more systemic and simulation-driven experience rather than a traditional action prototype. I wanted to explore how actors could make decisions based on needs, schedules, available points of interest, and resources in the world.

A major motivation was to practice building connected systems instead of isolated features. AI actions, homes, workplaces, restaurants, parks, resources, and carriers all needed to interact in a way that felt coherent and readable.

Result & Reflection

The project evolved into a city-simulation prototype where NPCs can work, rest, eat, move between locations, and react to changes in the world state. The most valuable result was not a single mechanic, but how multiple systems began supporting each other.

The biggest lesson was how important architecture becomes once many systems depend on shared world state. Refactoring POI logic and resource logic into dedicated managers made the project easier to reason about and much easier to extend.

AI Architecture

NPCs use a Brain system together with actions such as Eat, Sleep, Work, Relax, and carrying resources. Decisions are influenced by needs, daily schedules, and what is currently available in the world.

This let me build behavior that feels systemic rather than scripted, where actors adapt based on world state instead of following fixed sequences.

POI & Resource Management

A POIManager handles homes, workplaces, restaurants, and parks, while a ResourceManager owns world resources, including spawning, reservation, delivery logic, freshness, and decay.

Moving these responsibilities out of GameWorld was one of the most important refactors in the project and made the simulation significantly cleaner.

Needs, Schedules & Simulation Flow

Actors are driven by needs such as hunger and energy while also following a time-of-day schedule. This creates a readable daily rhythm where NPCs move between home, work, restaurants, and leisure locations.

A key challenge was making these systems stable and debuggable while many actors were running simultaneously and affecting the same world state.

What I’d Improve Next

If I continued the project, I would improve pathfinding robustness, expand debugging visualization, and deepen the simulation layer so actors affect each other more directly instead of mainly reacting to resources and schedules.
